Youtube is a very helpful option. Here are some helpful channels: School of Cardistry, CardMechanic, Bao Magic, lotusinhand, etc.

If you are just starting out, here is my recomended way of progressing. Basic grips -> Riffle Shuffle -> Swivel Cut -> Revolution Cut -> Dribble, Basic Two Handed Cuts(Werm, Sybil, Kick Flip) -> More Advanced Two Handed Cuts -> Whatever you want to do! I promise that you won't regret it, because cardistry really is fun




















Cardistry is a very impressive and visually stunning art form that also requires amazing dexterity, hand eye coordination, practice, and more. To master cardistry means to master fine motor skills to the level of surgeons! Cardistry originates from magicians in the 19th flourishing cards to entertain and amaze the audience, and it is still popular among modern day magicians. However cardistry does not involve magic and has branched out from that field. Today there are cardistry conventions held all over the world for artists to express their skills. The most popular type of cardistry focuses on cuts, spreads, spins, shuffles, and other flourishes. People will mix these together into sequences and name them. In conclusion, cardistry is an amazing skill that you should definetely consider looking into!
